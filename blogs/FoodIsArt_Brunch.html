Food Is Art Brunch: A Feast for the Senses

The Old Orchard

The Food Is Art Brunch returned this past weekend and once again, it delivered an unforgettable experience that blended fine dining, soulful music, and South African excellence. Hosted at The Old Orchard, a breathtaking venue surrounded by panoramic mountain views, the afternoon unfolded under golden sunlight, celebrating the harmony between food, wine, and creativity.

Curated by Viwe, founder of Food Is Art, this second edition proved that culinary storytelling is indeed a form of art. Guests were treated to a luxurious 7-course culinary journey, each dish crafted with precision and paired perfectly by Tandokuhle, CEO of African Wine Bar, who led a captivating wine pairing education session that left guests both enlightened and inspired.

The menu featured an elegant progression from a beef rasher appetizer paired with Montpellier Spyseniersberg Red, to the garlic butter lamb chops paired with the same vintage favorite, and a dreamy finale of poached pear with mascarpone and caramel complemented by YBY Sparkling. Each course celebrated local flavors with a modern twist, beautifully matched with boutique South African wines.

Three expert barmen from African Wine Bar poured and presented each wine with grace and sophistication, adding a touch of theatre to every glass served. The experience was seamless refined yet warm, elegant yet deeply personal.

As the music played, the energy in the air shifted guests couldn’t resist dancing, their spirits lifted by the rhythm and the wine. The event was completely sold out, a testament to its growing reputation as one of the most distinctive culinary experiences in the region.

By the end of the afternoon, the chant was unanimous “We want more!” Many guests even purchased bottles of their favorite wines to take home, a true reflection of the success of the pairings and the emotional connection sparked by the event.

The Food Is Art Brunch once again proved that when food, wine, and creativity come together, magic happens. It was more than a meal it was a multisensory celebration of African excellence, artistry, and passion.
