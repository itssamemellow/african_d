African Diamond Wines & African Wine Circle Presents: Malcolm X Day

Home Suite Hotels, Rosebank

In the heart of Rosebank on February the 21st 2025, African Diamond Wines in collaboration with the African Wine Circle put together an exquisite event to celebrate the Life of Malcolm Little, popularly known as Malcolm X In what they namesake called “Malcolm X Day”

Set under a beautiful African sunset in Rosebank ; Home Suite Hotels Rooftop. The energy and aura of the evening illuminated with radiance and endless possibilities. The room glistened and the sun bounced off every piece of glassware setting a tone of luminous positivity.

Upon arrival guests received welcome drinks, courtesy of YBY, a refreshing Méthode Cap Classique (MCC) just to set the mood right. The room was full of Industry Leaders, Wine enthusiasts, Radio Personalities, Journalists, Influencers and so much more. The African Diamond team welcomed and ushered guests into a massage area that was brought to and curated by Black Scottish, a certified massage practitioner with an interest specifically in mobility and flexibility.

Mbali Sibeko the Master of Ceremonies called the room into attention, formally welcomed all the guests and introduced Cwenga Buhlungu, who serenaded the room in an acapella performance of and renditions for Miriam Makeba's Lakutshon'ilanga and Malaika. With each song, the singer explained the significance in relation to the theme of black history which informed and educated the room.

Thandokuhle Mpinda, the Founder and CEO of African Diamond Wines welcomed and introduced his guests to the love of his life, this wine brand has been a long time coming passion project that he's invested so much time, effort and energy into. Seeing it come into fruition feels like an out of body experience, He notes that it was not an easy journey, the trail wasn't always clear but he was adamant on creating and cultivating a Wine that after all is said and done will speak for itself in the room and no one can deny its quality.

Adetunji Omotola, founder of the African Wine Circle introduces his wine club and gave a brief back ground on how the African Wine Circle came to be and his extensive journey with wine over the years.

To speak more extensively about the wine and how it was made, Cezzane Kouta a distinguished Sommelier and winemaker himself walked guests through the basics of wine all the way through to its complexities. He taught and educated guests about the origin of Grenache and the significance of African Diamond Wines farm being in the Swartland region and why that eco-system works to their benefit.

The African Diamond Grenache Blanc was served first and Cezanne talked tasting notes, temperature and storing, the floor was opened to the guests for a Q&A session regarding the Grenache Blanc.

An array of African Cuisine canapés that took everyone on a culinary tour of Africa were served and the innovation was just incredible.



Guests were spoiled to:

  • Fried Plantain - Ghana
  • Suya Spice Beef & Cous Cous - Nigeria/Morocco
  • Peri-Peri chicken lollipop - Mozambique
  • Deconstructed Kota - South Africa


Suite Treat
  • Mikate (Puff Puffs)
    tossed in star anise syrup and coconut - Congo



As guest enjoyed the canapés beautiful music lingered in the background as everyone engaged about the paring of the food and the wine.

Towards the end of the refreshments session, the staff at Home Suite Hotels started serving the African Diamond Grenache Noir. Cezzane once again got up and walked the room through this beautiful wine and its creation. Guests marvelled at just how exquisite the Noir tasted and the floor was once again open for a Q&A session and Cezzane was more than ready to answer all their questions.

Adetunji Omotola was welcomed back on the stage and he proceeded to give the guests a master class on the life of Malcolm X and the significance of the date 21 February as it commemorates the day the Civil Rights Activist passed away. He overall gave a beautifully informative presentation that left guests insightful and more knowledgable about the plight of Malcolm X.

Letepe Maisela, Author of “The Empowered Native” He gave a talk about his experiences in exile and how he ultimately came back home to pick up the pieces of a scattered nomadic life. Mr. Maisela graciously gifted all the guests with signed and autographed copies of his book.

The formalities came to an end and the guests were encouraged to stick around, socialise and enjoy the African Diamond evening. The event was a success and many of the guests were proud to be affiliated with and to be invited by African Diamond to celebrate this auspicious occasion.
