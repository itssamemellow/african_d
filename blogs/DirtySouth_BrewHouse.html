Wine at the Brewery Down South

Corner Valley & National Road, Margate

The African Wine Bar hosted one of its flagship events, "The Wine Down at the Brewery", an intimate wine and food pairing evening at Dirty South Brewery on Thursday, 21 August. The night brought together guests for a celebration of flavour, culture, and connection, curated by founder Tandokuhle Mpinda, a proud South Coast local from Margate.

The evening began at 6:30 pm with a warm welcome before guests gathered around platters of Dirty South Brewery favourites. Tables were filled with the Cowboy T-Bone Steak, sliced and shared, alongside baskets of crispy hot wings and the brewery’s Mighty Basket, which brought together riblets, pap balls, samoosas, and other crowd-pleasers. The food set the tone for a relaxed, communal atmosphere where conversation flowed easily and guests connected long before the first glass was poured.

With plates cleared, the wine journey began. Guests first raised a glass of Yeah Baby Yeah (YBY) Crystal Dry, a crisp sparkling wine whose playful name captures a spirit of joy and celebration. This opening set the mood for the tasting and paved the way for the Montpellier Spyseniersberg Chenin Blanc, whose freshness paired naturally after the hearty platters. The third pour, Essay Red Blend, added layers of spice and earthy character, before the evening closed with the bold Montpellier Spyseniersberg Red, a wine that delivered richness and depth as a fitting finale to the experience.

Reflecting on the event, Mpinda said: "Sharing food before the tasting opened people up. It created a welcoming energy. From there, the wines were not just about tasting notes, they were part of a shared story."

As one of its flagship formats, Wine at the Brewery highlights the African Wine Bar’s vision of bringing people together around wine in settings that are warm, engaging, and approachable. Beyond public events, the African Wine Bar is also available to book as a private bar for special occasions, corporate functions, and intimate gatherings, offering a unique space where food, wine, and community come together.

The Margate event reinforced the African Wine Bar’s role in shaping memorable wine experiences on the South Coast and left guests looking forward to the next opportunity to raise a glass together at Dirty South Brewery.
