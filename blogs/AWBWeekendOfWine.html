African Wine Bar Hosts Weekend of Wine, Lifestyle and Wellness Experiences in Cape Town

1 Lower Bree Street

Cape Town, South Africa. African Wine Bar hosted a curated weekend of wine, lifestyle and wellness experiences at Protea Hotel North Wharf in the Foreshore, bringing together guests for a series of intimate and engaging events

The first event took place on Saturday, 28 February 2026 at the venue located at 1 Lower Bree Street. Guests began arriving from around 15:30, with the programme officially starting at 17:00. Founder of African Diamond and African Wine Bar, Tandokuhle Mpinda, welcomed attendees and introduced the evening’s wine tasting experience. Guests were guided through a curated selection of wines, with Mpinda sharing insights about each one throughout the evening.

Guests were encouraged to make use of scorecards provided at their tables, allowing them to rate each wine and engage with one another while enjoying the tasting.

The experience began with YBY bubbly, followed by Cronier’s Sauvignon Blanc. Breaks between tastings allowed guests to socialise and enjoy a harvest style table, creating a relaxed and inviting atmosphere.

The tasting continued with Cronier’s Cabernet Sauvignon and Montpellier’s Spyseniersberg, before concluding with a celebratory Montpellier MCC to close the evening. Guests enjoyed the music ambience and continued to mingle before the event concluded at approximately 21:00.

Luxury beauty brand Sisley Paris also hosted an activation during the event, offering makeup touch ups and curated goodie bags that allowed guests to enjoy an additional premium experience during the evening.




The weekend’s activities continued the following morning with the launch of Dolce Far Niente on Sunday, 1 March 2026. The event, whose name reflects the idea of “the sweetness of doing nothing,” introduced a wellness element to the programme.

Guests gathered from 09:00, with the session beginning at 09:30. The morning started with a guided pilates session led by Black Scottish, lasting approximately 40 to 45 minutes and offering participants a calm and energising start to the day

Following the session, guests enjoyed a glass of bubbly while relaxing and connecting with fellow attendees.

Organisers described the gathering as the first of many Dolce Far Niente experiences, designed to create moments of relaxation, connection and mindful enjoyment.

Together, the weekend’s events highlighted a growing appetite for curated lifestyle experiences that combine wine culture, wellness and hospitality in a welcoming and social environment.
